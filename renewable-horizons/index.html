Editorial Platform

Renewable Horizons

An editorial and research platform focused on renewable energy systems, distributed infrastructures, and the conceptual development of metabolic architecture.

Definition

What Renewable Horizons is

Renewable Horizons is the editorial platform associated with PRMS Architecture Lab. It operates as a space for research publication, theoretical exploration, and dissemination of knowledge in energy systems and architecture.

It precedes the formal consolidation of PRMS as an institutional research framework and constitutes the foundational body of work from which PRMS emerges.

Role in PRMS

Position within the institutional structure

Renewable Horizons functions as the editorial and conceptual engine of PRMS Architecture Lab.

Editorial Production

Development and publication of books, research documents, and conceptual frameworks.

Knowledge Base

Compilation of research that supports the theoretical and operational development of PRMS.

Conceptual Laboratory

Exploration of ideas related to energy systems, sustainability, and distributed infrastructures.

Dissemination Platform

Bridge between research production and public access to knowledge.

Book Collection

Research series (2021–2026)

Between 2021 and 2026, Renewable Horizons has produced a collection of 35 books focused on renewable energy systems and distributed sustainability models.

Focus

Solar systems, water systems, energy efficiency, passive design, and distributed energy strategies.

Scope

From introductory frameworks to applied technical guides and conceptual models.

Contribution

This body of work establishes the empirical and conceptual base that later evolves into PRMS.

Continuity

The collection remains active as a parallel editorial line within PRMS Architecture Lab.

Relation to PRMS

From editorial to framework

Renewable Horizons represents the initial phase of research and publication. PRMS Architecture Lab formalizes this knowledge into a structured, measurable, and scalable architectural system.

While Renewable Horizons focuses on publication and dissemination, PRMS Architecture Lab focuses on systematization, validation, and institutional research development.
