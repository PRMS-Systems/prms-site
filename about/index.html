About
PRMS

PRMS is not a product, a studio, or a certification. It is a research programme and theoretical framework — a 20-year intellectual project formalizing the idea that the validity of architecture is measured, not declared.

The System

What PRMS is

PRMS is a conceptual framework and operational methodology. It belongs to no institution. It is governed by its published principles, its formal taxonomy, and the data generated by the systems that implement it.

Its intellectual lineage is explicitly positioned within the history of architectural systems thinking — from Howard's Garden City and Le Corbusier's Five Points through Cedric Price's Fun Palace, Fuller's geodesic domes, and Japanese Metabolism. PRMS is the metabolic chapter of that lineage: the moment when architecture becomes measurable.

Its primary validation is SmartLiving Milenio III in Querétaro, México — a built prototype operating under continuous IoT instrumentation. Its PEAR-ROI Score of 0.78 (Year 1) is the first published empirical validation of PRMS at Level 3 — Autónomo.

Research Programme

The line of inquiry

PRMS emerged from a single reformulation: if the validity of a building can be measured, then the design process must begin with the measurement system — not the form. Everything else follows from that inversion.

Track A — Immediate Dissemination

Self-publication via LinkedIn, Zenodo, and personal platforms. Establishes authorship priority and builds audience prior to peer review. Active from 2025.

Track B — Academic Validation

Latin American indexed journals (Revista AUS, Bitácora Urbano Territorial). Full QRO-01 IoT dataset as empirical basis. Target: Q3 2026.

Track C — International Positioning

Elsevier/Scopus journals (Energy and Buildings, Building and Environment). Conference presentations at PLEA and SB. Book publication. Horizon: 2027–2028.
