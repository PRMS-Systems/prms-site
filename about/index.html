The Research Programme

About
PRMS

PRMS Architecture Lab is an independent research organization dedicated to the development, validation, and dissemination of measurable architectural systems. Within this institutional context, PRMS is developed as a research programme and theoretical model that formalizes the idea that the validity of architecture is measured, not declared. Established in 2024, the Lab serves as the institutional entity responsible for advancing, validating, and applying the PRMS framework. It operates from Querétaro, Mexico, and is supported by a distributed network of research collaborators and contributors.

The System

What PRMS is

PRMS is a conceptual framework and operational methodology developed and maintained by PRMS Architecture Lab. It is governed by its published principles, formal taxonomy, and the data generated by the systems that implement it.

Its intellectual lineage is explicitly positioned within the history of architectural systems thinking — from Howard's Garden City and Le Corbusier's Five Points through Cedric Price's Fun Palace, Fuller's geodesic domes, and Japanese Metabolism. PRMS is the metabolic chapter of that lineage: the moment when architecture becomes measurable.

Its primary validation is SmartLiving Milenio III in Querétaro, México — a built prototype operating under continuous IoT instrumentation. Its PEAR-ROI Score of 0.78 (Year 1) is the first published empirical validation of PRMS at Level 3 — Autónomo.
