PRMS — Arquitectura Metabólica Medible: Manifiesto y Diagrama Fundamental

Hernández García, O.A. — 2025 — Zenodo Repository

PRMS (Measurable Metabolic Architecture) is a distributed architectural framework that defines the minimum habitable unit as a measurable, autonomous node capable of producing, regulating, measuring, and deciding its own resource flows. The system is structured around four primary flows — energy, water, air, and data — understood not as isolated infrastructures but as continuous, interacting metabolic processes. These flows operate across scales, from the individual node to microgrids and urban systems, maintaining a consistent logic of distributed autonomy. Unlike conventional sustainability frameworks, PRMS introduces a measurable and operational approach to architecture, where performance defines form. The framework is supported by real-world prototyping and sensor-based monitoring systems (PEAR-ROI), enabling post-occupancy validation and data-driven decision-making. This document presents the foundational manifesto and the official PRMS diagram, establishing the conceptual, operational, and visual basis of the system. PRMS is proposed as an open, scalable, and measurable architectural system intended for validation, replication, and future standardization.

Preprint — Zenodo

Genealogía, Crítica y Superación Disciplinar: Posicionamiento Crítico Histórico del Sistema PRMS

Hernández García, O.A. — 2025 — Zenodo Repository

This document establishes the critical and historical positioning of the PRMS system (Performance Regulated Metabolic System) within the evolution of 20th and 21st century architectural thought. Through a structured genealogical analysis, it examines key movements — Japanese Metabolism, Archigram, Soleri’s Arcology, Passive House, and environmental certification systems such as LEED, BREEAM and EDGE — identifying a shared structural limitation: the absence of an operational, autonomous, measurable and scalable architectural unit. The PRMS introduces a fundamentally different principle: the metabolic node as a distributed, data-driven, and performance-verifiable unit capable of operating across all scales. Unlike previous systems that remained formal, speculative, centralized, or fragmented in evaluation, PRMS integrates real-time measurement, energy production, environmental regulation, and financial performance into a unified operational framework. This document does not present the full technical standard but establishes the disciplinary argument for PRMS as a new architectural paradigm based on measurable autonomy and systemic replicability.
